Prox (Capstone)

Prox - Rapid Information Propogation via Beacons

Project Summary

(Year long, independent HCI capstone project)

Most information in several neighborhoods and educational campuses is generally available in many different ways like flyers, bulletin boards or online like social media sites, mailing lists etc. but is often delivered very late or in a manner that is easy to ignore. Another bottleneck is that most often, users only pay attention or are limited to information relevant to their workplace/building/school etc. and might miss out on several fun/important activities being held elsewhere on the premises.

Inspired from Apple's iBeacon tech and location/context aware research, Prox is a design concept that aims at rapid and context aware information disimmination via a localized network of LE Bluetooth beacons. ‘Prox’ runs on smartphones as a native app and listens for incoming notifications when the user is in proximity of a beacon.

  • Conceptual Design
  • Personas and User Scenarios
  • User Research and Requirements
  • Gathering
  • Rapid Sketching
  • Participatory Design
  • Iterative Wireframing and Paper Prototyping
  • Cognitive Walkthroughs and User Testing
  • Iterative High Fidelity Prototypes
  • User Evaluation

Deliverable

A highly interactive prototype that has been refined over the course of the course timeline through multiple interations of design and feedback from real users, and detailed usability reports documenting the design process applied and findings from user evaluations.

Data Morphed Topographies

Data Morphed Topographies for Improving Precision with Touchless Interactions on Large Displays

Research Context

Large, high-resolution displays enable efficient visualization of large datasets. To interact with such visualizations, touchless interfaces can potentially support fluid interaction at different distances from the display. But touchless gestures lack haptic feedback, thereby causing users’ gestures to unintentionally move off interface elements and requiring additional effort to perform accurate actions. To mitigate this problem, we introduce Data-Morphed Topographies (DMTs): adaptive constraints on cursor movements that guide touchless interaction along the structure of visualized data. We present three topography primitives—holes, valleys, and pits—which map to common visualization primitives, such as nodes, lines, and regions. In a controlled comparison with unconstrained touchless input, participants were significantly more precise with DMTs, and did not perceive significantly more workload. The efficacy of DMTs was significantly greater in more difficult tasks. By morphing data visualizations into virtual topographies, our work is a crucial first step to improve the precision of touchless input.

Role in Research Team

  • Initial Ideation and Task Design
  • UI design and Research Prototype Development (C#, WPF)
  • Secondary Investigator for User Testing
  • Presented preliminary research concept at Ubicomp (Seattle, 2014)

Prior Research

This work is based on previous investigation and research design as illustrated in our submission at Ubicomp 2014: Holes, Pits, and Valleys: Guiding Large - Display Touchless Interactions with Data-Morphed Topographies.

Advocacy of Networks

Advocacy of Networks

Research Context

Online social networks can exist both explicitly, through a structured list of connections in one’s social network platform and also, implicitly, through the links that connect websites with one another. While an extensive amount of research exists on the user experience and value of explicit social networks, most research on implicit networks is limited and studies massive samples from the Internet or focuses on understanding large scale networks within a specific domain.

The proposed research aims to fill a significant gap in our understanding of online social networks by exploring the user experience of implicit social networks, that are rendered by one’s experience on the Internet, with a specific emphasis on philanthropic advocacy. The research will undertake two phases of empirical inquiry, bridged by a development phase. The first phase will comprise of semi-structured interviews with individual bloggers followed by longitudinal deployment of a technology probe with these users. Development of the probe for more targeted data collection will bridge the two phases.

Role in Research Team

  • Co-developer for the technical probe (visualiztion and web-crawler) and used technologies such as JavaScript, D3 (Data-Driven Documents) and MySQL database.
  • Assisted in paper writing

DressWiser

DressWiser - A Smart Mirror Assistant

Project Description

DressWiser is a smart-mirror concept that aims to serve as a personalised and effective assistant that helps overcome everyday dressing-up and related decision-making. With this goal in mind, the target audience includes all members of a household with an active lifestyle, average to high social interaction and who commute to work, school or social events. DressWiser is context and location aware and offers dressing recommendations, destination updates etc. based on the context of use and user's wardrobe. The “Primary” user group is young professionals and students, especially women (owing to a relatively larger wardrobe inventory to choose from). Users should ideally be somewhat familiar with smart device interaction using touch and gesture commands.

OLSM Dice (Arduino)

OLSM Universal Dice (Arduino Prototype)

Project Description

This 3 week project was geared towards learning and designing a physical product prototype using the Arduino redboard. The focus of the project was on physical protottyping skills and using Arduino components. )

A number of table top games use multi-sided dice and it can really be annoying to deal with a number of die, specially in games such as Dungeons&Dragons etc. Our solution was a univeral die concept that could be rolled as any X-sided dice (2,4,6,8,10,20 etc).

The retain the experience of rolling an actual die, the product was designed such that it would offer a novel, yet slightly familiar experience. This was facilitated by simulating the rolling experience with a flex sensor to detect and measure a roll, audio feedback and an affordance that the device could be held and even used as a prop in the game.

Design Process

  • Design Ideation
  • Sketching
  • Story boarding
  • Gathering
  • Physical Prototyping (with Arduino)
  • User Testing

Deliverable

An interactive physical prototype that sufficiently demonstrates the use of physical prototyping skiils, usage of arduino components and tested with some users representing the target user profile.

Wallfie

Wallfie - Interactive Photo Wall (Prototyping)

Project Description

This 3 week project was geared towards designing an interactive product prototype and while doing so, documenting the entire design process with a special focus at explaining design considerations, decisions and iterative prototyping to address key issues.

Wallfie is a large screen interactive photo wall, which can be used at the theme parks, malls' resting areas, or points of interests. The screen allows users to take personal and group photos with customizable selected backgrounds and accessories that can be selected and cycled through with gestures. To take a photo, all users on the screen say “Cheese!”. At the end of each session, the system prints a paper photo card with QR code that allows users to download their digital photo, and information how to receive a printed photograph. When no users are taking photos, the display defaults to a slideshow of photos of people who took pictures previously with the system.

Design Process

  • Sketching
  • Story boarding
  • Paper Prototyping
  • Cognitive Walkthroughs
  • Interactive prototyping using Axure (and HTML/CSS)
  • User Testing and Evaluation

Deliverable

An interactive physical prototype that sufficiently demonstrates the use of diverse prototyping skiils, a working interactive prototype refined over the course of the project duration with iterative testing and feedback.
